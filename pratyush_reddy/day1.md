# OWASP IoT Top 10: Quick Summary
1. Weak, Guessable, or Hardcoded Passwords: The device uses a terrible default password like "admin" that anyone can guess, and sometimes the manufacturer doesn't even let you change it.
2. Insecure Network Services: The device leaves unnecessary digital doors wide open to the internet for hackers to walk right through.
3. Insecure Ecosystem Interfaces: The device itself might be fine, but the mobile app or website you use to control it is totally unprotected.
4. Lack of Secure Update Mechanism: There is no safe or automated way to patch the device when a new bug is found, so it just stays vulnerable forever.
5. Use of Insecure or Outdated Components: The manufacturer built the gadget using old, recycled software parts that already have well-known security flaws.
6. Insufficient Privacy Protection: The device carelessly collects your personal information and habits without clearly asking for your permission.
7. Insecure Data Transfer and Storage: The device sends and saves your private data in plain text, making it incredibly easy for anyone on the network to snoop.
8. Lack of Device Management: Once the gadget is sold, the company has no system in place to monitor its health, push updates, or safely retire it.
9. Insecure Default Settings: The device comes straight out of the box with all the security features turned off, expecting you to figure out how to lock it down.
10. Lack of Physical Hardening: If a bad actor actually gets their hands on the physical device, they can easily pop it open to steal its internal secrets.
